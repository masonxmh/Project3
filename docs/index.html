Background

In late 2019, the outbreak and spread of Coronavirus sent the world into a panic. Thousands of people around the world get confirmed each day. This is the virus that can affect the respiratory system and cause pneumonia. A huge number of suspected patients began to go to various testing sites for testing. And at the same time, the lung testing departments of major hospitals are also overcrowded. In the United States, the speed of lung X- rays tests is limited. In an emergency, the results of a chest X-ray can be available within a short period of time. Otherwise, results are usually ready in 1 or 2 days. In most cases, results cannot be given directly to the patient or family at the time of the test. In this case, our team members decided to make a model of machine learning, by training pictures of normal patients and patients with pneumonia. Use this method to improve the speed of pneumonia diagnosis and treatment.

Base Model - VGG16

VGG16 is a convolutional neural network model. It was known as one of the best trained models, which contains 16 weight layers

Our Model

Model Architecture

We keep all the CNN layers from VGG16 by frozen the first 20 layers, remove the original output layers, then add 3 hidden layers(dense layer) for our model. Before training the model, the class weight has been adjusted for higher accuracy. Modelcheckpoint, ReduceLROnPlateau, and EarlyStopping are used in callback functions to save the model and prevent model overfitting.

Model Performance

From the model accuracy graph, the best model is obtained at epoch10, since the trained model starts to overfitting after this point. We only pick the model at epoch10, and discard the rest. Then this model was evaluated by test dataset, the result is:
Normal Neural Network - Loss: 0.009955005720257759, Accuracy: 0.9522998332977295.
